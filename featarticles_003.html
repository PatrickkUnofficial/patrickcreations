UNTAPPED POTENTIAL

A story about cocoa trees, waiting, and the tragedy of preparation without action
Written by Emmanuel Patrick Published on December 27, 2024 5 min read

I used to live close to a very large plantation. It was a cocoa plantation. The farmer told me he was the only one in the region who grew cocoa, explaining that it was very expensive to cultivate because it required substantial investment and cocoa takes a long time to mature before it can be harvested.

I had watched the cocoa grow from seedlings. I remembered when the farmer had just begun planting them, and now they stood as huge, towering trees. I felt proud just observing their growth, and I wondered how much prouder the farmer must have felt.

He had everything he needed to become the biggest cocoa exporter in the region. He was positioned to make millions in just a few years. The potential was undeniable—rows upon rows of valuable cocoa trees, each one a future source of wealth.

I waited. I waited for him patiently. I watched for signs of his success—for him to change his clothes, renovate his house, buy a fine watch, or drive a new car. But he never did. I waited and waited, season after season, but nothing ever happened.

Seasons turned to years, and the trees grew taller and stronger, yet the farmer remained exactly as he was.

I wondered what was wrong, so one day I walked up to his house and knocked on his door with a pie in hand. He opened the door and invited me in. We shared the pie, had a good laugh, and talked all day until the setting sun reminded me it was time to return home.

As I prepared to leave, I decided I should ask him while I still had the chance. So I did. "With all these cocoa trees," I asked, "why haven't you become the wealthy exporter everyone expects you to be?"

He looked at me thoughtfully, then simply said, "I'm not ready yet." That was all. He had worked all those years, nurtured the trees through their growth, but he simply wasn't ready.

I accepted that as my answer and left. So there the great money-makers stood—majestic cocoa trees, owned by this man who saw them not as opportunity, but as something he wasn't yet prepared to embrace.

The years continued to pass, and the man began to age. He grew older and older, his hair turning gray, his steps becoming slower.

He never did make his fortune from those trees. The potential remained exactly that—potential. Untapped, unrealized, waiting for a readiness that never came. He was simply never ready, and so the trees stood as monuments to what could have been.
